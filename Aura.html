Once upon a time, Filipinos expressed love through harana — through songs sung under windows, through letters written with trembling hands, through effort that spoke louder than fear.

I grew up with my grandmother, and maybe that’s why I still believe in doing things this way — carefully, sincerely, with intention.

I don’t know where to begin, or if it’s even okay for me to reach out like this. I don’t know if I’m still learning how to love again… or if I already know the answer.

But I do know this — I want it to be you.

I admire your style, your eyes, and your smile. The way you carry yourself, the way your outfits always seem to fit you so well. I’d love to keep talking to you — maybe someday you can even teach me how to dress properly.

So I’ll ask this gently. May I keep talking to you every day, until I find the courage to finally ask you out?

I spent my days relearning how to code just to make this. I hope, in its own small way, this speaks to you.

Sincerely,
Nems
